# Cellular Sandbox

A browser-based interactive cell biology visualization.
No download. No install. Open it and try not to let your cell die.

## What It Is

The cell is the first living machine. Everything before it —
chemistry, physics, energy — was infrastructure. The cell is
where those systems became something that breathes, responds,
divides, and fights to survive.

Cellular Sandbox makes that visible and interactive across
four tiers of depth.

## Tiers

**Tier I — The Living Cell**
A full animal cell rendered with every organelle in constant
subtle motion. Mitochondria flicker with ATP production.
The Golgi apparatus buds vesicles in real time. Ribosomes
drift through the cytoplasm. The nucleus breathes.

Click any organelle for its name, function, and — most
importantly — why it has the shape it does. Structure exists
because function demanded it. Every organelle in this cell
is the shape it is for a reason.

**Tier II — Organelle Deep Dive**
Individual organelles pulled out and rendered in full 3D.
Drag to rotate. Zoom in on mitochondrial cristae — the
folded inner membrane that exists entirely to maximize
surface area for ATP production. Watch the ribosome hold
an mRNA strand while a protein chain grows. See the nuclear
pores that control what enters and exits the command center.

Each organelle comes with its full structural explanation.
The why is the whole lesson.

**Tier III — Cell Types**
Five cell types. Radically different shapes. One reason
each looks the way it does.

The neuron has a meter-long axon because distance is its
entire job. The red blood cell has no nucleus because it
sacrificed everything for oxygen capacity. The muscle cell
has multiple nuclei and visible sarcomere stripes because
contraction is so expensive it needs genetic backup and a
mechanical structure you can see. The bacterium has no
membrane-bound organelles because it predates them by a
billion years.

Shape is story. Tier III is about reading it.

**Tier IV — The Living Sandbox**
Pick a cell type. Keep it alive.

Every action has an ATP cost. Feeding raises it. Hydrating
stabilizes the membrane. Duplication is expensive — it will
trigger full mitosis animation with spindle fibers, but it
costs 35 ATP. Starving drains the meter in real time and
the organelles slow visibly as energy depletes. Attacking
introduces a pathogen and you watch the membrane struggle.
Irradiating shows DNA strand breaks forming in the nucleus.
Mutating alters gene expression and leaves a mark.

An event log timestamps every action. The cell status
updates with every state change. The ATP meter is
everything. Watch it.

This tier was designed to feel like a video game. That's
intentional. The resource management, the cost-benefit
decisions, the scramble to recover — a real cell does all
of this. Every second. In your body right now.

## No Audio
Cellular Sandbox has no audio layer. The drama here is
entirely visual — a cell doesn't need sound to tell you
when it's dying.

Cellular Sandbox is part of a growing series of interactive
science visualizations designed to make complex systems
feel real and explorable. Each sandbox builds on the last.

- [EMS Sandbox](https://silent-pfil.github.io/EMS-Sandbox/) — live

- Neural Transmission Sandbox — in development
- Newtonian Sandbox — "Universe Sandbox"
- Einsteinian Sandbox — in development

The series starts at the molecular instruction set and
scales outward — through energy, through cells, through
networks, through planetary systems, to spacetime itself.
